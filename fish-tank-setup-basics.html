Fish Tank Setup Basics

A simple first-tank guide for families who want a cleaner, calmer, safer aquarium routine before bringing fish home.

Back to Fish Guides Open Starter Checklist

Best first rule

Set up the tank and routine before buying fish. A rushed tank can become stressful fast.

Fish safety note

This page gives general aquarium planning information. For sick fish, unsafe water, sudden deaths, or confusing tank problems, ask a qualified aquarium professional, aquatic veterinarian, or local fish store specialist.

Start simple

First tank setup checklist

Tank

Choose a tank that gives fish room to move and gives the family enough water volume to keep conditions steadier.

Filter

Use a filter made for the tank size. Check that water is moving and the filter is not blocked.

Conditioned water

Prepare water before fish go in. Do not use untreated tap water unless your setup is known to be safe for fish.

Thermometer

Know the temperature range needed for the fish type before buying fish.

Before buying fish

Make the tank ready first.

  • Pick the fish type before buying supplies.
  • Set up the tank, filter, water, and hiding places.
  • Learn how often to feed and clean.
  • Keep a simple water-change note.
  • Buy fewer fish at first, not more.

Family job

One feeder is safer.

Pick one person to feed the fish. This helps prevent overfeeding, cloudy water, and confusion about who already fed them.

Simple weekly notes

  • feeding amount
  • water changes
  • filter checks
  • temperature checks
  • fish activity changes
